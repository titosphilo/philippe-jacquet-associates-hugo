Trauma therapy is concerned with experiences that have not been fully integrated by the mind and body. A person may know that an event is over, yet continue to react as if danger remains present. This can affect sleep, concentration, mood, relationships, work, appetite, sexuality, alcohol or drug use, and the capacity to feel settled in ordinary life.

At Philippe Jacquet & Associates, trauma work is approached with discretion and clinical care. The aim is not to force disclosure or revisit painful material before there is enough stability. Therapy begins by understanding what is happening now: the symptoms, triggers, patterns of avoidance, bodily responses and the personal history that gives those reactions meaning.

EMDR may be used where traumatic memories, shock or intrusive responses remain active. For some clients, this can help reduce the charge held around particular memories or images. For others, trauma work needs a broader psychotherapeutic frame, especially where the difficulty is linked to childhood experience, repeated relational injury, addiction, eating disorders, shame or long-standing anxiety.

The work may include grounding, emotional regulation, careful exploration of memory, attention to dreams and symbolic material, and a gradual strengthening of the client's capacity to think and feel without becoming overwhelmed. This is particularly important for clients who have carried distress privately for many years, or who have remained highly functional while feeling internally unsafe.

Trauma therapy can also involve grief. Sometimes the traumatic wound is not only what happened, but what could not happen afterwards: being believed, protected, understood or allowed to recover. A serious therapeutic relationship gives those missing dimensions space to be named and worked through.

Appointments are available in London and online. The practice offers a confidential setting for people who want a thoughtful, private and clinically grounded approach to trauma rather than a generic or overly simplified intervention.
