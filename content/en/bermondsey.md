Bermondsey sits at the edge of two worlds — the City’s pressure to the north, the quieter residential streets spreading south. People who live and work here carry a wide range of things: the strain of professional lives that look successful from the outside, the longer-standing difficulties that move with you wherever you go, and the specific kind of exhaustion that comes from keeping it all contained.

Philippe Jacquet and Associates has a practice in Bermondsey offering the same depth and quality of clinical work as the Harley Street office — without the journey across the river. Sessions are private, unhurried and entirely confidential.

## What the Bermondsey Practice Offers

Individual psychotherapy draws on integrative and Jungian frameworks — work that takes seriously not just what you present with, but what underlies it. For those carrying trauma, EMDR is available: Philippe Jacquet is an EMDR practitioner with over 20 years of experience, and it is used here as part of a broader therapeutic relationship rather than as a standalone protocol.

The practice also offers specialist work with addiction, eating disorders, art psychotherapy, and bereavement counselling. All of these are available in Bermondsey, not only at the London W1 address.

## Who Comes to the Bermondsey Practice

People come here for many reasons — a pattern of anxiety that has run alongside professional life for long enough that it no longer feels unusual, a relationship that has become difficult in ways that conversation alone cannot seem to shift, an addiction that has moved beyond a problem into a way of managing something deeper, a grief that was never properly mourned.

Some have tried therapy before. Others are arriving at it for the first time, having reached a point where something needs to change.

The practice works with adults. Sessions are available at times that accommodate working schedules.

## Getting Started

An initial consultation in Bermondsey is the appropriate first step. It is a conversation — not an assessment, not a commitment — in which you can speak about what has brought you here and sense whether this is the right place to continue.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How is the Bermondsey practice different from Harley Street?

The quality and depth of clinical work is identical. The location is more accessible for those living or working in South London — SE1 and the surrounding areas.

### Do I need a GP referral?

No. This is a private practice. You can contact the practice directly and arrange an initial consultation without any referral.

### Is everything confidential?

Yes. All sessions are conducted under strict confidentiality. Nothing shared in sessions is disclosed to any third party without explicit consent.
