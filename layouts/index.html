Philippe Jacquet & Associates is a private psychotherapy, counselling and analytical practice based in the heart of London, with appointments available from Harley Street, central London and online for international clients. The practice works with people seeking serious, confidential and intellectually rigorous help for anxiety, trauma, eating disorders, addiction, relationship difficulty, professional pressure, depression, identity questions and deeper losses of meaning.

The clinical approach is integrative. It brings together advanced professional training, Jungian analytical psychology, EMDR for trauma, CBT where appropriate, and close attention to the particular history of the person in the room. This is not a manualised service in which one method is applied to every client. The work is designed to understand symptoms, repetitive behaviours and inner conflict within their psychological, relational and biographical context.

That makes the practice especially suited to clients who want more than immediate symptom management. Some people come because a crisis has made change urgent; others because an older pattern has begun to repeat through the body, relationships, work, sexuality, alcohol, food or emotional life. Therapy can then become a place of clarification, containment and lasting transformation. The setting remains discreet, professional and attentive to complex lives often lived under considerable pressure.

Sessions are available in English and French. The practice is particularly experienced with clients who value privacy, depth and a careful therapeutic relationship: professionals, executives, internationally mobile clients, couples and individuals who may have tried brief intervention before and now need a more substantial psychological conversation.

La pratique clinique de Philippe Jacquet s'appuie sur plus de vingt-cinq ans d'experience dans le champ de la psychotherapie, du counselling, du traitement des troubles alimentaires, de l'addiction, du trauma et du travail analytique. Son parcours associe une formation professionnelle approfondie, une experience clinique de longue duree et une recherche doctorale orientee vers la pratique. Le titre DProf marque cette articulation entre expertise clinique, reflexion critique et developpement professionnel avance.

Philippe Jacquet est accredite EMDR Europe, ce qui permet d'integrer un travail specifique sur le trauma lorsque les symptomes sont lies a des evenements identifiables, a des experiences relationnelles repetees ou a des formes plus diffuses d'inscription traumatique. L'EMDR peut etre particulierement utile lorsque le corps continue de reagir comme si le danger etait present, meme lorsque la situation actuelle est objectivement differente. Ce travail est integre avec prudence dans une comprehension plus large de la personne.

Le cabinet est egalement marque par une orientation jungienne. Cette perspective permet de travailler avec les reves, les images, les conflits inconscients, les repetitions relationnelles et la recherche de sens. Pour certains clients, la question centrale n'est pas seulement de supprimer un symptome, mais de comprendre ce que ce symptome exprime, protege ou repete. L'approche analytique ouvre un espace dans lequel le symptome peut devenir intelligible sans etre reduit a une simple erreur de fonctionnement.

Les consultations se deroulent dans un cadre prive et confidentiel, en anglais ou en francais. Le cabinet recoit des clients de Londres, du Royaume-Uni et de l'international: personnes confrontees a une crise personnelle, professionnels soumis a de fortes responsabilites, clients ayant deja entrepris une therapie, ou individus souhaitant commencer un travail psychologique plus profond. L'objectif n'est pas de proposer une image ideale de la vie psychique, mais d'aider chaque personne a developper plus de liberte, de discernement et de capacite d'action.

{{ else }}

Philippe Jacquet's clinical practice is grounded in more than twenty-five years of work across psychotherapy, counselling, eating disorder treatment, addiction, trauma and analytical psychology. His background combines substantial professional training, long clinical experience and doctoral-level practice research. The DProf reflects that connection between clinical expertise, critical reflection and advanced professional development, rather than a purely academic distance from the realities of therapeutic work.

Philippe Jacquet is EMDR Europe accredited, allowing trauma work to be integrated when symptoms are connected to identifiable events, repeated relational experiences or more diffuse forms of traumatic imprint. EMDR can be especially useful where the body continues to respond as if danger is present, even when the immediate situation has changed. Within the practice, this work is approached carefully and placed inside a wider understanding of the person, their history and their current psychological resources.

The practice is also shaped by a Jungian analytical orientation. This creates room for dreams, images, symbolic life, unconscious conflict, relational repetition and the search for meaning. For some clients, the central question is not only how to remove a symptom, but what the symptom is expressing, protecting or repeating. Analytical work allows the symptom to become intelligible without reducing it to a mechanical fault or an isolated behaviour.

Consultations take place within a private and confidential frame, in English or French. The practice sees clients from London, across the UK and internationally: people in personal crisis, professionals carrying significant responsibility, clients who have previously tried therapy, and individuals beginning deeper psychological work for the first time. The aim is not to offer an idealised picture of mental life, but to help each person develop greater freedom, discernment and capacity for action.
