There is usually a moment — sometimes quiet, sometimes acute — when a person realises that what they have been doing is no longer working. The strategies that once managed the difficulty, the habits that kept things contained, the ways of being in the world that once felt like choices: they stop functioning as reliably as they once did. Something is asking to be looked at.

This practice exists for that moment.

About

A different kind of practice

Dr Jacquet is an integrative psychotherapist and Jungian analyst with over 25 years of clinical experience in London and internationally. He is an EMDR Europe-accredited practitioner at the highest level, a trained art psychotherapist, and an addiction specialist who trained at the Hazelden Foundation and Hope-One. He holds a Doctorate of Professional Practice — the only person in Europe to have completed doctoral-level research specifically on male eating disorders. He trained as an executive coach at ESSEC Business School, ranked among Europe’s top ten by the Financial Times.
